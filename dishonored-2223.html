Dishonored 2223

Escape from the Cyber Prison · 09.2023 - 10.2023

Description

Dishonored 2223 is a level design project built in Unreal 5.1. The goal of this project is to recreate the Prison Escape experience from the original game (Dishonored 1) in a world where human guards have been replaced by cameras and machines.

Story:

A sword is no longer useful here, but the player has a powerful Staff that lets her leap over gaps and "climb" up high walls.

The platform used is Unreal 5.1 and the toolkit is called Level Up Toolkit. Link to toolkit

Paper Design / Level Explanation

Game Mechanics

The "POLE" (Affordance)

A pole can let players do special movement: such as slide or hang on the hooks. Players can use these 2 skills to help them pass some specific terrain or some traps.

Tutorial level: learn how to use the pole.

Breakable Structures / Walls

In the level, there will be some breakable structures or walls. I use light color to highlight these structures so players are aware of them at key points. The green cube (affordance) in the scene can also break the walls.

Red color walls indicate the breakable structure.

Damage Zone

In the level, there are many different kinds of damage zones: Cameras, Damage floor, Traps. All of these damage zones will reduce the player's health. If health drops to 0, the player will die.

Cameras, Dead Trap, Damage Floor.

Other Triggers

There are other interactable triggers in the level:

  • Buttons: used to open all the doors in the level. Sometimes, players need to find the hidden button for the doors in the map.
  • Recovery Zone: players can get healing at this zone. Indicated by green colors.
  • Portal: enter one door and exit from another.

Rough Prototype Playtest

After I built a rough prototype of my level, I invited 2 players to playtest. Here are the notes during the playtest and their feedback.

Level / Map Iterations

Button / Door

From the playtest feedback, I added blue wires to connect the buttons and the doors. Now players will understand that buttons are used to open doors in the room. The wire also acts as a leading line, guiding the player's attention through the level.

After

A button behind the breakable wall.

Lighting Effect

At some points, players could not see the breakable structure and would miss the correct path. According to the feedback, I added a spotlight and changed the color of the wall.

Before: Dark and Grey

After: More obvious, Orange color

Before: Too dark to see the path

After: Added more lights as direction

Landmark / Direction

From players' feedback, I added a landmark for direction at the end of the level. This helps players know where the final destination is and makes their "mission" clearer. This position is also a vantage point where players can decide whether to continue to the building or keep exploring the map.

Landmark at the end of the level, also known as Vantage Point.
